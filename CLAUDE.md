## Architectural binding decisions (PCD v4 + Sprint plans)

These bind every implementation decision; do not re-litigate without surfacing back to Keith.

- **LOFAR is linear-frequency, NOT mel-scale.** PCD v4 §6.6.
- **Calibrated uncertainty is first-order (platform-layer moat).** Deep ensemble + conformal prediction. PCD v4 §5.1; Phase 1 deliverable.
- **Vessel-level holdout for splits.** Recording-level splits leak. PCD v4 §12.2. The DeepShip published 98.58% likely uses recording-level splits; we don't compete with that number, we compete on equivalent splits.
- **Service-oriented internal structure from Day 1.** Even single-process Phase 0 separates modules with typed Pydantic interfaces. PCD v4 §7.1.
- **Platform vs Tuor capability split.** Five capabilities are Fathom platform substrate (ingestion, fusion, track maintenance, COP infrastructure, open API); six are Tuor product (LOFAR/broadband, line detection on LOFAR, classification, multi-array bearing intersection, IUSS operator workflow, operator confirm/reject loop). PCD v4.3 §6. Code under `src/fathom/` reflects this split logically; physical reorg deferred.
- **Audit sidecars on every artifact.** Provenance is non-optional. PCD v4 §7.1.
- **Phase 1 exit technical validation gate (PCD v4.3 §15.2).** Platform play is committed. Three sub-gates: (1) classification on real ambient data — operationally credible, "better than nothing and improving with data"; (2) calibrated uncertainty — deep ensemble + conformal, ECE < 0.05, coverage tracks alpha; (3) platform composability — second pipeline consumes Fathom services without modification. **Leading Product 2 candidate: USW DSS (AN/UYQ-100) displacement on surface combatants (DDG/CVN, FY27 MAC IDIQ vehicle).** Airborne ASW second; cable protection opportunistic only. DEMON removed as platform capability (deferred to whichever product needs it). Capability count: 11.
- **Three-surface Tuor UX architecture (validated 2026-05-22).** Alert Feed (analyst tool, alongside LOFAR gram -- priority-ranked detections, click to investigate); Sensor Map Overlay (replaces ICP map half -- geographic view with bearing lines, cross-correlation, ambiguity resolution, potential fixes); Watch Supervisor Dashboard (operations tool for Watch Sup / TACCO -- all contacts, pending review, sensor coverage, activity log, re-association proposals). Every frontend/UX decision must specify which surface it targets. PCD v4.3 §6.4.
- **360-beam continuous monitoring is the operational scale requirement.** Each IUSS array has 360 beams (180 independent look directions + 180 reciprocal due to left-right ambiguity in linear arrays). Tuor monitors all beams continuously -- the core value proposition over ICP where operators walk beams manually. At 180 independent directions, per-beam FAR must stay below ~0.03/hour to keep aggregate analyst alert rate at 5-10/hour. This makes calibrated uncertainty a hard operational requirement for the product to function, not just an exit gate metric. PCD v4.3 §6.10.
- **Reciprocal-beam pairing and cross-array correlation are built-in noise reduction.** Beam N and beam N+180 see the same signal; detection on both = confirmation. Cross-array correlation: same contact on different arrays confirms and enables bearing intersection for fix. Tiered confidence: multi-array correlated (high, surface immediately), reciprocal-pair confirmed single-array (medium, surface), single-beam only (low, log but don't surface unless high SNR). PCD v4.3 §7.2.
- **SBIR Phase I is trade studies and analyses, not prototypes.** Prototype development is Phase II. Ulmo enters Phase I having already built what the proposal studies -- the trade study we'd propose is "here's what we've demonstrated on unclassified data, here's the analysis of how it transfers to the classified domain." Do not frame sprint priorities around SBIR demo readiness. The SBIR proposal writes itself from the engineering results.
- **No classified data in unclassified environments, ever.** `.gitignore` tripwire patterns are belt-and-suspenders; partition discipline is the real defense.
- **Tuor is a single autonomous-watchstander agent** (Agent Architecture Reframe 2026-05-25, PCD v4.4 §7). Holds the entire AOR — every beam and every sensor — in one world model. Single agent over per-beam agents: per-beam fragments the cross-beam awareness that defines competent watchstanding. Five faculties: perception (eyes; Sprint 6 shipped), world model (Phase 2), attention (Phase 2), judgment (Phase 2), action+feedback (Phase 2). Fathom = the cognitive layer + framework that any undersea operator-agent inherits ("ROS for undersea autonomy"; external phrasing leans "cognitive layer" or "operating system for undersea autonomy" for program-office audiences). Replaces the §7.2 feed-forward pipeline framing.
- **Dual-path detection is permanent** (Manifesto Ch 7.4; PCD v4.4). Classical TPSW + ML U-Net run together; agreement between paths = high-confidence detection. Classical path IS the adversarial-ML defense and the deterministic cross-check. Disagreement lowers confidence rather than raising a false alarm. Sprint 7 D wires this in.
- **Phase 1 exit FAR criterion is binary ON COVERAGE, not absolute FAR** (team disposition 2026-05-25). Adaptive conformal must restore coverage tracking within finite-sample bounds on cross-domain calibration set. Order-of-magnitude FAR reduction is directional secondary. The 5-10/hr operational floor is explicitly Phase 2/3 data-scale, not a Phase 1 gate. This framing avoids the false-precision trap of "are we 100x or 10x off after Sprint 7?" — measures the algorithmic improvement directly.
- **Data path is public cabled-observatory, not self-collected** (Build Plan 2026-05-25). Self-collection dropped for cost/time. Calibration ambient = ONC Oceans 3.0 continuous recordings (target hydrophone: Strait of Georgia for VTUAD-source alignment). Cross-domain classification = VTUAD (108K 1-sec clips at IEEE DataPort DOI 10.21227/msg0-ag12; impl repo lucascesarfd/underwater_snd) routed to Cluster F. MBARI Pacific Sound + OOI low-frequency hydrophones in Phase 3. **Coastal cabled data is NOT IUSS-distribution** (open-ocean deep-water multi-element-beamformed) — public pivot fixes exchangeability for the conformal question; distance to IUSS ambient closes only with cleared access.
- **AIS-assisted suppression does NOT move per-beam FAR** (team disposition 2026-05-25). Per-beam FAR is the rate of false detections over BACKGROUND beams (no ship per AIS); AIS deprioritizes detections where a ship IS. Two separate levers: per-beam FAR moves only with calibration + representative negative data; AIS lowers operator effective alert rate by explaining away true detections of uninteresting traffic. Phase 2 AIS work targets the latter, not the former.
- **Metrics change with architecture per phase.** Phase 1 metrics (F1, ECE, recall, coverage) measure the perception faculty. Phase 2 metrics (operator-equivalent: contacts held vs analyst, aggregate alert rate, contact-hold time, time-to-detect) measure the agent. The metric set changing between phases is the reframe working — flag this to reviewers as design, not inconsistency.

## IUSS operator workflow and Tuor design reference (elicited 2026-05-22)

Full IUSS watch-floor workflow mapped from CEO operational memory. This is the design reference for all Sprint 6+ capabilities.

**Workflow chain:** Watch start (formal turnover briefing, active contacts on front-room screen) → Sensor assignment (WESTLANT/EASTLANT AOR, 1-3 operators per sensor segment, two Watch Sups one per AOR, TACCO leads floor) → Search steady-state (operator walks beams, single gram full-screen, manual visual search for persistent horizontal lines) → Detection + initial assessment (mental map against active contact vulnerabilities, check frequency/bearing/known acoustics) → Reporting ("LOI on Array XX, Beam XX at XXXXZ" over intercom) → Escalation fork (friendly WESTLANT: Watch Sup + TACCO approve; hostile: preliminary mark, sent to CUS for confirmation) → Contact establishment (contact number, classification, check against contacts tracked within 48-72 hrs) → Track maintenance (bearing annotation on strongest array/beam, routine 2-4 hrs, high-priority 30 min, ICP computes position from manual bearing inputs) → Cross-sensor correlation (manual judgment: "is the signature on sensor X the same as on sensor Y?"; once associated, ICP does automated bearing intersection geometry) → Contact loss / re-acquisition (entirely operator judgment; ICP has zero automated re-association).

**ICP pain points (all confirmed by CEO):** Coverage gaps (not enough eyes on enough beams), tedious manual work (beam walking, bearing annotation, correlation), system limitations (slow, no automation, no re-association, no environmental modeling beyond bathymetry terrain mode, no propagation prediction, no weather/ambient noise awareness).

**Tuor displacement path:** Autonomous watchstander is the wedge. Tuor monitors all 360 beams continuously (what operators cannot do manually). Progressive workflow coverage drives ICP displacement: detection augmentation first, then tracking automation, then cross-sensor correlation proposals, then environmental awareness. Each step earns more operator trust and absorbs more ICP workflow.

**Compute feasibility at 360-beam scale:** STFT (~2,800 FFT/sec across all beams) handles on single CPU; U-Net inference at 6-30 inferences/sec (single to ensemble) handles on single GPU; memory ~720 MB for rolling gram buffers. Compute is tractable. FAR at scale is the hard problem -- the gating constraint is calibrated uncertainty, not compute.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Don't reproduce song lyrics or large copyrighted excerpts from source datasets.
- Don't propose using mel-scale, log-mel, or any perceptually-weighted frequency representation for operator-facing grams.
- Don't ship classified data or secret-tagged files. Don't `git add` files matching the tripwire patterns even if Keith requests it.
- Don't commit without an explicit Keith green light.
- Don't add framework dependencies (FastAPI, MLflow, Hydra, etc.) preemptively. Add when first needed.
- Don't conflate platform and product. "Fathom" refers to the platform substrate; "Tuor" refers to Product 1 (IUSS-shore watch station). Future products will earn their own Tolkien names; don't pre-claim them.
- **Don't `git add -f <directory>` on artifact dirs that might contain `.pt` or other large gitignored files** (Sprint 6 B.8 lesson). `git add -f` on a parent directory OVERRIDES the `*.pt` gitignore rule and pulls binary blobs into history. Sprint 6 B initially landed 2 of 5 ensemble `best.pt` files (~720 MB) before `git reset --soft HEAD~1` cleanup. Pattern: stage explicit per-file source paths (`git add scripts/foo.py src/fathom/bar.py`); for artifact directories, use `git add -f artifacts/<specific-dir>/` only when you've confirmed no `.pt`/`.pth`/`.ckpt` present (`git diff --staged --name-only | grep -E "\.(pt|pth|ckpt)$" && echo STOP || echo "clean"` before commit). Sprint 5 pattern (no `.pt` ever committed) is the standard; the Sprint 6 lapse was a one-time correction.
- Don't conflate signature transfer with calibration shift (Sprint 6 D / Sprint 7 A team lesson). Synthetic-tonal recall on foreign ambient measures the AMBIENT-shift effect on calibration (the conformal question — Cluster A/B/C). It does NOT test whether the model detects real foreign vessel signatures — that's a classification cross-domain question (Cluster F with VTUAD's clip-level vessel labels). Don't read low synthetic-tonal recall as "model doesn't transfer"; that conclusion needs real-label data.
- Don't conflate VTUAD with continuous-recording datasets (Sprint 7 A team correction). VTUAD is 108,588 one-second clips curated by distance band — IEEE DataPort DOI 10.21227/msg0-ag12, NOT Kaggle; "Vessel Type Underwater Acoustic Data," NOT "Vessel Tracking." Impl repo: lucascesarfd/underwater_snd. It cannot feed the 256-frame patch pipeline (1s < 33.15s patch floor). For patch-pipeline calibration ambient, use ONC Oceans 3.0 continuous recordings (the source VTUAD was cut from), not VTUAD itself.
